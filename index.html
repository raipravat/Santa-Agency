Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about life insurance in Nepal

In Nepal, you can choose from several types of life insurance including:

  • Term Life Insurance: Provides coverage for a specific period (10, 15, 20 years)
  • Whole Life Insurance: Permanent coverage that lasts your entire lifetime
  • Endowment Plans: Combines insurance with savings, paying out after a set period
  • Money Back Policies: Provides periodic payments during the policy term
  • Unit Linked Insurance Plans (ULIPs): Combines insurance with investment opportunities

A good rule of thumb is to have coverage that's 10-15 times your annual income. However, the exact amount depends on:

  • Your current income and future earning potential
  • Outstanding debts (home loan, personal loans, etc.)
  • Your family's living expenses
  • Children's education costs
  • Existing savings and investments

I provide free personalized assessments to help determine your ideal coverage amount.

The claims process typically involves these steps:

  1. Notification: Inform the insurance company immediately
  2. Documentation: Submit required documents (death certificate, policy documents, ID proof)
  3. Verification: The company verifies the claim
  4. Settlement: Payment is made to beneficiaries (usually within 30 days)

I assist families through the entire claims process to ensure smooth and timely settlement.

Life insurance premiums in Nepal can be paid:

  • Annually: Single payment each year (usually offers a discount)
  • Half-yearly: Two payments per year
  • Quarterly: Four payments per year
  • Monthly: Through ECS (Electronic Clearing Service) from your bank account

Most insurers offer flexible payment options. I help clients choose the payment frequency that best suits their financial situation.

If you miss a premium payment:

  • Grace Period: Most policies have a 15-30 day grace period
  • Policy Lapse: After grace period, policy may lapse but can often be revived
  • Revival: Lapsed policies can typically be revived within 2-5 years by paying missed premiums with interest
  • Reduced Benefits: Some policies offer reduced paid-up insurance if not revived

I proactively remind clients about upcoming payments and assist with revival if needed.
